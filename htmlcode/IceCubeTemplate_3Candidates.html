Introduction to ICXXXXA

On MJD 58668.82 the IceCube Neutrino Observatory reported the detection of the highly-energetic neutrino IC19xxxxA (GCN ....). Due to the high-energy and the track-like signature the event has a good positional uncertainty and is likely of astrophysical origin. With the goal of identifying the corresponding electromagnetic counterpart we report here on the multi-wavelength dissection of the region around the event. A full report (in .pdf format) can be accessed HERE.

This page provides a synthetic overview of the outcome of our automatic pipeline processing, which consists of a) a search for blazar and other possible candidates counterparts using the VOU-BLazars tool, b) a detailed analysis of the Fermi-LAT gamma-ray emission around the IceCube error region in a temporal window close to the neutrino detection and the full Fermi data set, and c) the production of the broad band SED and of the light-curves of the most promising candidates.
